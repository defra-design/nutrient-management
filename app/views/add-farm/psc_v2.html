What is the phosphorus sorption capacity (PSC) of the soil in Top field?

Help with Phosphorus sorption capacity (PSC)

The phosphorus sorption capacity (PSC) of soil is its ability to bind with applied phosphorus.

If you are unsure about the PSC for your soil, the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA) provides a map of soil PSC indices.

View the map (opens in a new window)

You can also read Scotland's Rural College (SRUC) Technical Notes TN715 to TN718, and TN770 (specific to your area) on the Farm Advisory Service Website.

View the SRUC technical notes (opens in a new window)

If you are still unsure, use PSC Index 1.
