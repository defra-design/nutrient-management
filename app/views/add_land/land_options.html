Do you want to enter details of any low risk run-off land?

You can spread slurry or poultry manure on fields that have a low risk of run-off, to reduce the amount of storage capacity you need to provide.

About low risk run-off land

Low run-off risk land is land that:

  • has an average slope of less than 3 degrees
  • does not have land drains (other than a sealed pipe)
  • is at least 50 metres from a watercourse or conduit leading to a watercourse

The NVZ minimum manure storage requirement may be reduced by the volume of slurry or poultry manure that can always be applied to land with a low risk of run-off following the end of the closed period.

This is provided that you:

  • comply with all other NVZ rules, including the requirement not to spread more than 50 cubic metres per hectare of slurry, or 8 tonnes per hectare of poultry manure in a single application between the end of the closed period and the end of February. You must allow at least 3 weeks between each individual application during this period.
  • mark the areas of low run-off risk land on your farm risk map for spreading organic manures.
  • provide, as a contingency arrangement, additional storage capacity equivalent to one week's worth production of manure. If you make any entry here, the tool will automatically calculate this extra requirement.
