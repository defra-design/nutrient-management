The NVZ minimum storage requirement can be reduced by the volume of slurry or poultry manure that can always be applied to land with a low risk of run-off following the end of the closed period.

This is provided you comply with all other NVZ rules, which means that:

  • you must not spread more than 50 cubic metres per hectare of slurry, or 8 Tonnes per hectare of poultry manure in a single application between the end of the closed period and the end of February. You must allow at least 3 weeks between each individual application during this period
  • you must mark the areas of low-risk run-off land on your farm risk map for spreading organic manures.
  • you must provide, as a contingency arrangement, additional storage capacity equivalent to one week's worth production of manure. If you make any entry here, the tool will automatically calculate this extra requirement
